Unit 1.1: Design for Humans

Toy: Step 1 -- Inspiration

Due: 16 March

Description

As we discuss human-centered design and the manufacturing process, you will be designing a toy and developing a prototype. To create the prototype you will use the shop tools and a variety of materials including wood, plastic, and recycled/reclaimed materials.

Procedure

Your first step will be to choose who you are designing the toy for. You can choose to make a toy for any age. The next step will be to learn about your audience; if you know people in your chosen age range, you should talk to them. You will next research toys that may or may not meet the needs in your target audience that you have identified.

About

Class meets period 4 in the Bentley Makerspace.

Class taught at Bentley School by Joey Knapp.

Course Description

This course is the third in a series of engineering seminars that focus on the design process and robotics. These are topics that many students have limited exposure to, but a high interest in. In this class, students will complete projects that will prepare them for work in college engineering, science, and math courses and a future career path. Topics in this class include programming, electronics, fabrication, computer aided design and manufacturing, and This class introduces students to product development and deployment. Students learn how to test and improve products along several constraints including cost, ergonomics, and sustainability. Students will also practice technical and scientific styles of writing, as well as presenting their ideas to others.


Learning Objectives
  • Develop their ability to collaborate with peers.
  • Practice creative problem solving by applying mathematical and scientific knowledge to novel problems.
  • Implement the design process.
  • Develop organizational and technical writing skills through the use of a design notebook.
  • Practice communicating complicated or technical concepts to a broader audience.
  • Understand the variety of factors designers and engineers consider while developing a product.
  • Use the design process to improve a product.
  • Program an Arduino microcontroller.
  • Use a CNC router for fabrication.
  • Design and build a robotic system to solve a problem.
